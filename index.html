About Me

I'm a seasoned engineering leader. I enjoy building high-performing teams and scalable, robust software. I program in Scala, Typescript, and Python, with a deep appreciation for type safety, observability, and comprehensive testing.

I'm driven by a curiosity for how complex systems work, whether in software or in society. Outside of my professional life, I'm passionate about improving democratic systems. As the former chair of the Center for Election Science, I've dedicated years to advocating for better voting methods.

Based in London, UK, I'm always open to connecting with interesting people. If you'd like to chat about technology, social choice theory, or engineering mentorship, feel free to book a 30 minute chat. I ask for a donation to the Center for Election Science to support their work.
